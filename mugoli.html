Mugoli Samba is an artist, journalist and dj born and raised in Ottawa, Canada. She has always been curious about the everyday people who pushed and pulled the tides of histories as we know them today. In its earliest stages, the curiosity birthed OTwaddup, a now defunct blog that documented Ottawa's Black youth art scene; an archive she hoped would help future Black artists in her hometown understand that they had always been here. She later organized two artistic showcases with local artists across mediums. The curiosity now manifests itself as a budding journalism career that she uses to unearth stories she hopes will shed light on lives like her own—diasporic, Congolese—in the complex story of Canada.

She is currently using the journalistic tradition of research to create art that investigates, preserves and propels history, notably as a DJ and as a multimedia artist. Her personal interests include listening to, and compiling stories of her Bafuliiru family and community in Ottawa, which informs some of her works in progress.

Mugoli graduated with a combined degree in Journalism and African Studies from Carleton University. She is currently a multiplatform reporter with Radio-Canada, Canada's francophone public broadcaster.
